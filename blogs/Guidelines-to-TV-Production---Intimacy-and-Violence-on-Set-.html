Guidelines to TV Production - Intimacy and Violence on Set

When it comes to TV production, there are certain guidelines that need to be followed, especially when it comes to intimate and violent scenes. These scenes can be extremely sensitive and require careful handling to ensure that everyone on set feels safe and comfortable. In this blog post, we will discuss some guidelines that should be followed during intimate and violent scenes.

Firstly, it is essential to establish clear boundaries and guidelines with the cast and crew before shooting any scenes that may involve intimacy or violence. This should be done through a detailed conversation that lays out expectations and ensures that everyone understands their rights and the boundaries that they can set. The conversation should also highlight the steps that will be taken to ensure that everyone on set feels safe and comfortable, including the use of intimacy coordinators.

Secondly, it is crucial to ensure that the cast and crew have a safe space to retreat to if they feel uncomfortable or overwhelmed during the shooting process. This can be done by creating a “cooling off” period where actors can take a break and regroup before returning to the scene. Having a safe space to retreat to and a designated person to talk to can make a huge difference in helping the cast and crew feel safe and supported.

Thirdly, it is important to use appropriate language and behavior during intimate and violent scenes. This means that any physical actions should clearly be choreographed and that performers should have prior rehearsal time to ensure that they are comfortable with the movements. It also means that language used during these scenes should be respectful and in line with the needs of the story.

In conclusion, it is crucial that intimate and violent scenes are handled with care and sensitivity. The above guidelines should be followed closely to ensure that everyone on set feels comfortable, supported, and safe during these scenes. The use of an intimacy coordinator and the creation of a safe space can make all the difference in the world, as can an open and honest dialogue about boundaries and expectations. By taking these simple steps, we can create a safe and supportive environment for everyone involved in the production.
